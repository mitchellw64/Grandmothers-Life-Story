Lynn Johnson

An insight into the world's most empathetic, loving, and kind mother and grandma.

Meet Cecilia Lynn Wescott Johnson! Born in 1935 in New Jersey to parents Gayle and Allen Wescott, Lynn is currently a mother of three and a grandmother of eight with current residency in Palm City, Florida, and New Rochelle, New York. With her loving and open-minded attitude, Lynn has consistently been a peacemaker and an angel towards everyone she meets.

In-depth history of Lynn Johnson:
  • 1930: University of Colorado graduate Gayle McGrimmon welcomes today’s guests as a cruise line hostess. While on her way back to the kitchen, she is stopped by one of the guests, MIT graduate and General Motors Executive Allen Wescott. Through a lot of talking, an unbreakable relationship is formed.
  • 1933: Gayle and Allen get married on June 20, 1933. Gayle changes her last name to Wescott.
  • 1935: Gayle and Allen’s first child, Cecilia Wescott, is born in New Jersey. Later on, Cecilia determines that she prefers her middle name, Lynn, over Cecilia.
  • 1937: Lynn’s younger sister, Pat, is born in New Jersey.
  • 1937-1950: Over the next few years, Allen and his family are forced to move internationally to Japan and India due to demands at General Motors. While Allen was at work, Gayle would often show Lynn and Pat the greatest sightseeing spots wherever they lived. In this time, Lynn grew a strong passion for Japanese architecture and art, which will influence her love for traveling the world later on in her life. Eventually, the family landed in their more permanent residency on the eastern shore of Virginia, on the “DelMarVa” peninsula.
  • 1944: Lynn’s younger brother, Tom, is born in Virginia.
  • 1953-1957: Lynn attends William & Mary, a small public research university in Williamsburg, Virginia. The university is the second oldest in the United States, being founded before the country became independent from Britain. Throughout these amazing four years, Lynn would become very well known, as she worked her way up to becoming the president of her sorority, Delta Delta Delta.
  • 1957: Lynn graduates with an Associate’s Degree in Interior Design at William & Mary.
  • 1957-1959: After college, Lynn visits a small interior design school in New York City.
  • 1959: During her time at the interior design school, Lynn went on a few double dates. On a double date between her current boyfriend and one of Lynn’s friends, she met her friend's date, John Johnson Jr. He was a Navy veteran and at the time, an employee at the advertising company Benton and Bowles Inc. Despite Lynn liking her current boyfriend, she couldn’t help but get feelings for John. Coincidentally, John felt the same way. So a few days later, Lynn and John broke up with their dates and began an everlasting relationship with each other. John decides to go by the nickname Buddy. Six months later, Lynn and Buddy rented an apartment together in the city.
  • 1960: On February 6, 1960, Lynn and Buddy get married on the Eastern shore of Virginia. Lynn starts working at General Reinsurance.
  • 1962: Lynn and Buddy’s first son, Bill Johnson, is born. Lynn decides to quit her job at General Reinsurance to become a full-time mom for her kids.
  • 1964: Lynn and Buddy’s second son, Jimmy Johnson, is born. The Johnson family then moved to Scarsdale, New York.
  • 1968-1972: While Lynn’s children were in about first to third grades, she volunteered to be a room mom in elementary schools, reading books for the kids in guest visits.
  • 1969: Lynn and Buddy’s first daughter, Gayle Johnson, is born.
  • 1974: Lynn volunteered as a girl scout leader for Gayle. Additionally, Lynn and Buddy moved to a different house in Scarsdale to accommodate the children’s needs.
  • 1978: Lynn joined a Japanese-American friendship group, which helps Japanese women who migrated to New York City and do not speak English well. If you would like to learn more about the organization, click the New York Times article here.
  • 1980-1982: Over the past decade, Buddy has been teaching golf to Bill, Jimmy, and Gayle. Lynn, although not loving golf herself, would often join their foursome and hit every so often, creating a nice medium for her and Buddy. However, she did love the human relations side of golf. On many weekends while the kids were occupied, Lynn would volunteer setting up tournaments for the nine-holers at Scarsdale Golf Club.
  • 1983: The Johnsons join Winged Foot Golf Club, a high-end club in Mamaroneck, New York.
  • 1984: Loving the previous nine-holers volunteer work, Lynn volunteers for the nine-holers at Winged Foot too. 1984 was also the year that the US Open, a major professional golf tournament, was hosted at Winged Foot. Lynn did not hesitate to support the US Open through volunteering as a cashier for tickets.
  • 1990: Around the time that Gayle was in college, Lynn and Buddy got an apartment in Harbor Ridge, Florida.
  • 1999: Loving the warm atmosphere of Florida, Lynn and Buddy decide to sell their Harbor Ridge apartment and buy a house in a golf community, still located in Harbor Ridge. Lynn would often enjoy her summers swimming in her pool and relaxing by the river, which runs through her backyard and stretches across the golf community.
  • 2004: Lynn and Buddy decide to move to a gated community in New Rochelle, New York. They keep their Harbor Ridge house and vacation there over the summers.
  • 2005-2021: Over the next decade, Lynn continued to happily travel between her homes in New York and Florida, as well as traveling the world to Japan, China, Singapore, Italy, and Colombia. After all the travelling, she was constantly celebrating the milestones of her grandkids as they progressed through school, college, and early job careers. Lynn was also always following world events and supporting the causes she believed in.
  • 2022: Lynn Johnson is currently a resident of New Rochelle and Palm City. She has three very talented and prospering children in finance and real estate. She also has eight grandkids, most of which are out of college living successful careers in finance, engineering, insurance, and psychology. Lynn lives happily with her husband of 62 years. Lastly, Lynn continues to enjoy Japanese art, good food, good books, reading the news, and time with her family and friends.

Author's note: Lynn, thank you for creating an environment of welcome, peace, and serenity for all friends and family you have positively affected. We are all grateful of your constant motivations for us to strive in the workplace and beyond. You are an amazing role model and a peacemaker for all. Thank you!
